The Principles of Design

Unity

Unity is the sense of harmony or cohesion in a design; how well everything fits together.

Variety

is the contrast or diversity in a design, and is created by juxtapositioning elements that don't quite match one another.

Emphasis

refers to an element's attention-grabbing quality, or lack thereof. Emphasis is created by focal points, which are the main points of interest in a design.

Balance

Balance refers to the weight distribution of a work of art.

Proportion

Proportion refers to the size of an element in relation to the sizes of other elements.
