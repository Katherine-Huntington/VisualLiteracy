Space

There are two kinds of space: white space and black space. Black space is space that's taken up by objects or other elements, such as text. On the other hand, white space is empty space. The ratio of black to white space in your design can have a variety of effects, so you need a good balance of both. Too much black space where it doesn't belong can make your design cluttered and difficult to read, while too much white space can make the design look bland or lazy.

To better visualize what I mean, here are three iterations of a design, with different black to white space ratios. Notice how the first and third designs both look like they were slapped together in two minutes, while the second one looks sleek and meticulously put together. The only thing that's different is the spacing between each element.

A good amount of white space is important for any design, from flyers to adverts to entire websites. Too little of it can make a design difficult to digest. Think of white space like chewing; it's pretty difficult to swallow an entire apple at once, but if you break it up into tiny pieces, you can eat the whole thing very easily. In the same way, white space breaks up your content so that it's easier to analyze, and is also vital for creating focal points in your design.
