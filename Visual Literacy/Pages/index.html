Visual Literacy

Visual Literacy is a student-led project dedicated to providing graphic design advice in the hopes that users can effectively communicate their message or advertise their product through the use of good design.

Elements and Principles of Design

In order to design something that looks nice, you first need to know about the major elements of design, which include the following: line, color, value, space, shape, form, and texture. No matter what you're looking at, the seven elements are always there. Just as letters are the building blocks of sentences, and just as cells are the building blocks of living creatures, the elements of design are the building blocks of vision; without the blocks, the build cannot exist. Therefore, it's important that we understand all seven of these elements if we want to create an effective design that communicates a desired message.

Once you understand the elements of design, you must also know how to use them effectively. Building blocks can't just be thrown together, however; that would result in a big, ugly pile of bricks that nobody wants to look at. And if nobody wants to look at your flier, sign or website, then you've largely failed to communicate to your audience. In order for building blocks to construct a good build, then, they must be laid out strategically. Just as letters must be strategically arranged to build the word “bumfuzzle,” and just as cells must be strategically arranged to build a dolphin's brain, the elements of design must be strategically arranged to build a particular design. These arrangement strategies are known as the Principles of Design. If the Elements are the building blocks of a design, then think of the Principles as the blueprint.
