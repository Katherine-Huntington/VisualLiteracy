About This Website

Visual Literacy is a student-led project dedicated to providing graphic design advice in the hopes that users can effectively communicate their message or advertise their product through the use of good design. Everything has been coded from scratch in HTML format.

The purpose of a visual design is to communicate with others. In order to communicate effectively, one must consider two things. First, the design should be aesthetically pleasing. Second, the aesthetics should reflect the message of the design. For example, the color palette for a bake sale poster would probably be much different than a color palette for a horror movie poster. As such, this website was created not only to teach people how to create aesthetically pleasing designs, but also how to choose the right kinds of aesthetics to match their intentions with a design.

The website isn't quite everything I wanted it to be; there are a lot of points that I didn't have time to touch on, and a lot of articles that are not yet accompanied by visual examples. Still, I hope that people will still be able to learn something new and apply that knowledge in their own designing efforts.
